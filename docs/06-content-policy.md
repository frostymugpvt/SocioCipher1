# SocioCipher — Content Policy

> **Version:** 1.0 | **Date:** 2026-05-11  
> **Effective for:** All users, all content types (posts, comments, replies, room messages)

---

## 1. Platform Stance

SocioCipher is built for **honest, open human conversation** — including on topics that are sensitive, uncomfortable, controversial, or divisive. We believe that the suppression of legitimate speech is itself a harm.

At the same time, we recognize that some content causes direct, concrete harm to individuals or society — not by being offensive or disagreeable, but by being genuinely dangerous, illegal, or designed to harass, exploit, or destroy.

**Our approach:**
- We err on the side of **allowing** speech when in doubt
- We act decisively on content that crosses clear harm thresholds
- We do not treat discomfort, offense, or disagreement as grounds for removal
- We do not apply political, ideological, or cultural bias in enforcement

---

## 2. Prohibited Content — Zero Tolerance

The following categories result in **immediate removal** without appeal:

### 2.1 Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)
Any sexual content involving minors, including:
- Explicit imagery or video (real or AI-generated)
- Sexualized text descriptions of minors
- Grooming solicitation targeting minors
- Links to CSAM hosted elsewhere

*Action: Immediate removal. Account suspension. NCMEC CyberTipline report. Law enforcement referral.*

### 2.2 Direct Threats of Violence
Credible, specific threats against an identifiable individual or group, including:
- "I will kill [specific person]"
- Calls for others to commit violence against a named target
- Detailed threats with location, timing, or method described

*Disagreement, hyperbole ("I could strangle this guy" in obvious venting context), and fiction are distinct and reviewed contextually.*

### 2.3 Terrorism Promotion and Incitement
- Content promoting, glorifying, or recruiting for designated terrorist organizations
- Incitement to mass violence or acts of terrorism
- Operational planning assistance for attacks

*Academic discussion, journalism, and counter-extremism analysis are distinct and allowed.*

### 2.4 Weapon and Illegal Goods Trafficking
- Solicitation or facilitation of illegal weapons sales (unlicensed firearms, illegal modifications)
- Drug trafficking coordination (selling, buying, distribution logistics)
- Human trafficking facilitation
- Stolen goods or fraud services marketplace

### 2.5 Doxxing
- Publishing another person's real name, address, phone number, workplace, or identifying information without their consent
- Aggregating publicly available information to create an identifying profile intended to facilitate harassment
- Sharing information specifically to enable others to harm, harass, or stalk a person

### 2.6 Non-Consensual Intimate Content (NCII)
- Sharing intimate or sexual images of a person without their consent
- Threats to share such content (sextortion)
- AI-generated intimate depictions of real, identifiable individuals

### 2.7 Coordinated Harassment Campaigns
- Organized, sustained targeting of a specific individual or group with the intent to intimidate, silence, or harm
- Brigading (organizing external groups to flood a person or room with abuse)
- Using multiple aliases to evade blocks and continue harassment

### 2.8 Fraud and Phishing
- Impersonating official services, institutions, or individuals to steal credentials or money
- Phishing links or social engineering schemes
- Cryptocurrency scams, fake investment schemes, romance fraud

### 2.9 Illegal Transaction Facilitation
- Advertising or coordinating transactions for any goods or services illegal in the platform's operating jurisdictions
- Money laundering assistance

## 3. Prohibited Content — Enforcement Required

The following result in **content removal and potential account action** but are reviewed by a human moderator before final action:

### 3.1 Targeted Personal Harassment
- Repeated, sustained abusive messages targeting a specific alias
- Content designed to humiliate, degrade, or intimidate a specific person
- "Pile-on" behavior where a group coordinates to overwhelm one individual

*Distinction: Criticizing someone's ideas, even harshly, is not harassment. Repeatedly calling someone subhuman, threatening their safety, or coordinating others to attack them is.*

### 3.2 Hate Speech — Dehumanizing Content
- Content that dehumanizes people based on protected characteristics (race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, national origin)
- "Dehumanize" specifically means: calling for their elimination, comparing them to subhuman entities, or denying their basic humanity
- Slurs used as attacks against individuals or groups

*Distinction: Criticism of ideologies, religions, practices, or communities is allowed. Calling for the elimination or degradation of a group of people is not.*

### 3.3 Impersonation
- Claiming to be another user, public figure, or platform official in a way that deceives others
- Creating content that falsely attributes statements to real people
- Operating an alias designed to look identical to a known alias to deceive

*Satire and parody with clear labeling ("parody account") is permitted.*

### 3.4 Spam and Inauthentic Behavior
- Automated posting or scripted content generation without human authorship
- Repetitive identical or near-identical content across multiple posts
- Coordinated inauthentic campaigns (multiple accounts posting the same agenda)
- Unsolicited commercial promotion

## 4. Sensitive but Allowed Content

The following content is **allowed** but may be tagged as sensitive or require an acknowledgment click:

### 4.1 Controversial Political Speech
- Criticism of governments, political leaders, political parties, ideologies
- Advocacy for legal policy positions regardless of how controversial
- Political satire, even sharp or offensive satire
- Discussions of election integrity, immigration, abortion, gun rights, taxation, etc.

### 4.2 Religious Debate and Criticism
- Criticism of religious doctrines, practices, institutions
- Atheist or anti-religious argument
- Inter-religious debate and disagreement
- Religious advocacy and proselytizing (non-deceptive)

### 4.3 Dark Humor and Satire
- Comedy involving dark, morbid, or uncomfortable topics
- Satire of public figures, institutions, or events
- Absurdist or transgressive humor that does not target specific individuals with abuse

*Assessed contextually. The same words can be satire or harassment depending on context and targeting.*

### 4.4 Mental Health Discussion
- Open discussion of mental health struggles, including suicidal ideation in a peer support context
- Substance use experiences (non-promotional)
- Trauma sharing and survivor narratives

*Crisis resources are auto-prepended to posts with crisis-related keywords. Content is not removed.*

### 4.5 Graphic Descriptions in News/Documentary Context
- First-person accounts of violence, trauma, war, or atrocity
- Journalistic reporting on disturbing events
- Documentary or historical discussion of atrocities

*Must be clearly framed as reportage/discussion, not glorification.*

### 4.6 Adult Themes (Non-Explicit)
- Discussion of sexuality, relationships, and adult topics in non-graphic form
- Debate around sex work policy, LGBTQ+ topics, reproductive rights
- Age-appropriate discussion of mature themes

*Graphic sexual content between adults is restricted to explicitly-labeled sensitive rooms and requires opt-in.*

## 5. Gray-Area Handling Framework

When content falls between clear zones, moderators apply this framework:

| Question | If Yes → | If No → |
|----------|---------|---------|
| Does this target a specific real person with abuse? | Lean toward removal | Lean toward allowance |
| Is there a victim who can be identified and harmed? | Lean toward removal | Lean toward allowance |
| Is this coordinated or one-off? | Lean toward removal | Lean toward allowance |
| Could a reasonable person interpret this as satire/opinion? | Lean toward allowance | Lean toward removal |
| Does removal suppress a legitimate political/social viewpoint? | Lean toward allowance | Lean toward removal |
| Is this content illegal in the operating jurisdiction? | Mandatory removal | Context-dependent |

**When genuinely uncertain:** Senior moderator reviews. No junior removal on gray-area content.

---

## 6. Ephemeral Content and Policy Enforcement

Because all content auto-deletes after 7 days, moderators must act within the content's active window for policy enforcement to be meaningful.

For severe violations (Tier 1–2), the preservation hold system ensures evidence is retained even after the 7-day expiry — for safety review and legal compliance — but this content is not shown publicly.

---

## 7. Policy Evolution

This content policy will be updated as:
- New harm vectors emerge (e.g., new AI-generated content abuse patterns)
- Legal requirements change across jurisdictions
- Community feedback surfaces enforcement gaps or overreach
- Moderation data identifies high false-positive rate in any category

All material policy changes will be:
- Published 14 days in advance
- Communicated via in-app notification
- Documented in a public changelog
